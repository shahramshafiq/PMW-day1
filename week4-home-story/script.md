# Twenty Guys and a Bus Ride

### Voice-over script | Individual Storytelling: What Home Means to You | Shahram Shafiq
**Runtime target:** 1:45–2:00 min at a slow, deliberate reading pace
**Angle:** something I miss now (Cadet College Hasan Abdal, 2019–2022)

---

> For three years, home wasn't a house. It was a wing, in a hostel, at Cadet College Hasan Abdal.
>
> I got there in 2019. Everything ran on a schedule so tight it should have felt like a punishment — wake-up, drill, class, games period, prep, lights out — but somehow it didn't. It felt like the safest routine I've ever lived in. Inside that routine, I found basketball, and I got good at it. Good enough that it became mine, the one hour of the day that was actually just for me.
>
> The best memories weren't even at CCH itself. They were on the bus rides to other cadet colleges for fixtures. I remember going to Cadet College Kallar Kahar, and it felt like a vacation from CCH, even though we were still in uniform, still representing our college. New campus, new faces, a team to cheer for, a game to win. Then back on the bus, back to the wing, back to the twenty guys I'd entered with — my entry mates, my wing mates, the ones who were there for every 5 a.m. wake-up and every late-night talk after lights out.
>
> I left in 2022. CCH only offered sciences at A Level, and I wanted to study CS and business, so I moved to Roots International. It was the right call. But three years later, I still measure routine, discipline, and friendship against that wing.
>
> That's what I miss. Not a building. Twenty guys, a basketball court, and a bus ride to Kallar Kahar.

~249 words.

---

## Why this structure

A real short film needs a beginning, middle, and end, not a list of facts. This follows that shape:

- **Beginning:** names the place plainly, home wasn't a house, it was a wing.
- **Middle:** the routine and basketball (what made it feel safe), then the one vivid scene, the bus ride to Kallar Kahar (what made it feel alive).
- **Turn:** the real, specific reason for leaving, not a vague "things changed."
- **Ending:** returns to the opening claim and makes it concrete: not a building, twenty guys, a basketball court, a bus ride.

## Every fact in this script

Every detail came directly from Shahram, nothing was invented to fill gaps:
Cadet College Hasan Abdal, 2019–2022, a disciplined hostel routine, basketball (learned there, excelled at it), away fixtures at sister institutes, specifically Cadet College Kallar Kahar, roughly twenty entry mates and wing mates, leaving in 2022 for Roots International Schools and Colleges because CCH only offered sciences at A Level and he wanted to study CS and business.
